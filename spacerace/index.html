The Cold War

was a period of
geopolitical tension between the Soviet Union and the United States, and their respective allies

It was an ideological war: both the Soviet Union and the United States tried to convince the world that their ideology, Communism or Capitalism, was better than the other.

The two global superpowers never fought directly against each other; it's why the war was called
The Cold War

The Major Events of
The Cold War included:

  • The Berlin Airlift
  • The Cuban Missle Crisis
  • Proxy wars
  • The Nuclear Arms Race
  • The Space Race

The first nuclear weapon was created by the United States during the Second World War and was developed to be used against the Axis powers.

In the years shortly following WWII, the United States held a monopoly on specific knowledge and raw materials for nuclear weapons.

American leaders hoped that their sole possession of nuclear weapons would help to secure agreements from the Soviet Government, but this proved to be unsuccessful.

Behind the scenes, the Soviet Government was working on building its own atomic weapons.

On August 29, 1949, the first Soviet bomb was detonated. It surprised the whole world, and especially the United States.

Now that both countries have nuclear weapons, an arms race developed.

Both countries invested massive quantities of time and money to increase the quality and quantity of their nuclear arsenals.

This was the beginning of

  • psychological warfare
  • propaganda campaigns
  • espionage
  • rivalry at sports events
  • technological competitions such as
    the Space Race.
